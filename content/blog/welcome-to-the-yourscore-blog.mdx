Every fan believes they know the game better than the person next to them. The pub argument, the group chat pile-on, the "name his last five clubs" test on the walk to the ground. Football runs on people backing their own knowledge.

YourScore exists to settle it. Daily football quizzes where speed matters, 38-0 where you build an all-time XI and take it head-to-head against the world, and leagues where you and your friends find out, over a whole season rather than one lucky guess, who actually knows their football.

This blog is the reading end of that. Here's what you can expect from it:

## What we'll write about

- **The stories behind the questions.** Every quiz we publish starts with something real: a record, a transfer, a moment that gets argued about years later. Some of those deserve more than one question.
- **Records and the people who hold them.** The numbers fans quote from memory, and the ones everybody gets wrong.
- **The big debates.** Every day there's a debate running on YourScore where thousands of fans pick a side. We'll dig into the ones that split the room.
- **How to get better.** What separates a decent quiz score from a great one, and what the sharpest players on our leaderboards do differently.

## Who this is for

Fans. Not pundits, not stat accounts. People who love the game, watch it every week, and want somewhere their knowledge counts for something.

If that's you, the games are free at [yourscore.app](https://yourscore.app). Read something here, reckon you'd have scored well on it, then go prove it.
